Is trekking in monsoon safe?
Yes, monsoon treks are usually safe. At Indiahikes, we only run those treks in monsoon that are safely navigable in the rains.

You will cover a total of around 26 km in five trekking days, during which you will gain approximately 5,100 ft (8,965 ft to 14,065 ft). Every day will include ascents and descents on uneven trails. The river crossings on this trek can be especially challenging.
Terrain:
On the first day of your trek, you cross a gushing stream over a wooden log to reach Jobra campsite. During peak monsoons, this can feel a bit nerve-racking.
The trail to Jobra climbs through forests. In heavy rain, this trail can get slushy and slippery.
Ahead of Jobra, you cross a stream over a narrow wooden log again right at the start of your day’s trek.
Just before Jwara, you need to cross the Rani Nala on foot. The water is biting cold and can reach upto your waist. Your Trek Leader and Guides shall help you navigate this crossing by forming a human chain.
The final day of ascent from Balu ka Ghera to Hampta Pass is challenging. You traverse multiple ledges and ridges, trekking continuously on bouldery moraine sections.
The descent from Hampta Pass is extremely steep and has a lot of switchbacks. This can get slippery in heavy rain. You can expect a lot of snow here, even in June.
The final day of your trek from Shea Goru starts by crossing the ice-cold glacier melt water of Hampta Nala. There are other stream crossings near Chhatru as well but you’ll find wooden bridges placed over most of them. However, in June and July, there may be snow bridges on which you can walk across the streams.
Please note: If you are trekking with Indiahikes, our Trek Leaders and Technical Guides will provide you with all necessary guidance, support, and technical gear such as micro spikes, gaiters, helmets, ropes, etc. wherever required.
Concerned about River Crossings on the Hampta Pass trek? Download the Ultimate Monsoon Guide to Hampta Pass now.
Weather:
Rainfall or snowfall, lasting for hours, can change the entire trek dynamic.
In June, when there is a lot of snow on the trail starting from Balu Ka Ghera, the trail can get slippery. However, this can be managed with the right gear like microspikes and gaiters.
In July and August, rain is a common occurrence. It makes the trail slippery and tricky. Moreover, the force of water at river crossings can increase, making it challenging to cross them.
Please note: The safety of our trekkers is paramount. Our technical team, in conjunction with the Trek Leader, constantly monitors the weather conditions and makes informed decisions about proceeding with or aborting the trek. If the weather deteriorates, the team may need to turn back from any campsite to ensure the safety of all trekkers.
Altitude:
The Hampta Pass trek is situated at 14,065 ft above sea level. At altitudes above 10,000 ft, the chances of being hit by Acute Mountain Sickness are real. Altitude sickness can happen to anybody, first-time trekkers as well as experienced trekkers. Fitness helps in making your trek easier. But do not forget that even a fit person climbing too high, too soon runs an equal risk of developing AMS.
Here’s a Complete guide to Acute Mountain Sickness, HAPE and HACE
Please Note: The Indiahikes team is equipped with high-altitude medical kits, oxygen cylinders, and stretchers to handle any altitude-related emergencies.
Emergency Exits:
Hampta Pass, being a Pass-crossing trek, has limited exit points. If you haven’t crossed the pass, then head down Jobra before moving on to Manali.
After crossing the pass, you’ll have to make it to Chhatru before heading to Manali via Rohtang Pass.
Closest Hospital to the Hampta Pass Trek:
Mission Hospital in Manali is the closest one to Hampta Pass, in case of a medical emergency during the trek. Manu Diagnostics Center and Hospital, also in Manali, is another option.
The Indiahikes team will ensure your safety throughout the journey. However, we believe that safety is a shared responsibility. We need your active participation and attention to safety protocols to ensure a safe and successful trek.

Q: Is trekking in monsoon safe?
A: Yes, monsoon treks are usually safe. At Indiahikes, we only run those treks in monsoon that are safely navigable in the rains.
We keep a close watch on weather patterns and trail conditions every single day. In unforeseen situations, we take necessary action — whether it’s rerouting, rescheduling, or even cancelling the trek if absolutely needed, keeping your safety in mind. It is always our first priority.

3. Clothes
Wearing layers is the mantra in the mountains. Layers give you maximum protection from all elements. And when the weather changes in the mountains (as it happens every few hours), you take off or put on layers as required.

Wear one T-shirt and carry two. Carry full sleeve dry-fit T-shirts (preferably collared). These prevent your arms and neck from getting sunburnt. In the rarified air on the trek, especially at high altitudes, UV rays can burn you in no time.
