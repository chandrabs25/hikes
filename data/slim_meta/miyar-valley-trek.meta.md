```text
Day time: Between 18 °C and 20 °C | Night time: Will drop to temperatures between 10 °C and 5 °C.

#fff

Day time: Between 15 °C and 18 °C | Night time: Will drop to temperatures between 5 °C and 0 °C.
```

```text
Moderate
Suitable for Fit Beginners
You will cover a total of around 60.2 km in six trekking days. Although you gain only 1,638 ft (11,564 ft to 13,202 ft), every day will include ascents and descents on uneven trails. The days are long and you will be trekking for 7-8 hours daily.
Terrain:
Long days are the biggest challenge on the trek. Almost every day, you’ll cover 9 to 10 kilometers. This requires you to trek 7-8 hours daily. This is no mean feat at 12,000 ft above sea-level.
Right after you step out of Khanjer, a steep uphill climb awaits you. It leads to a high-altitude cricket ground, your gateway into the Miyar Valley. This initial ascent can be quite tiring. Please pace yourself properly in this section
There are several river crossings on this trek. Palpu, where you camp on the fourth day of your trek, is situated in a river delta area. You need to cross a stream to get to this campsite and the current can catch you off-guard.
Even ahead of Palpu, you have two more stream crossings to navigate before reaching Kesar Yon Choo. These are glacial streams and the water is freezing cold. Going barefoot into these streams demands physical and mental preparedness.
There are boulder sections on this trek as well. You need to hop, skip, and balance across them. This can get tricky if you don’t have strong legs.
Please note: If you are trekking with Indiahikes, our Trek Leaders and Technical Guides will provide you with all necessary guidance, support, and technical gear such as micro spikes, gaiters, helmets, ropes, etc. wherever required.
Weather:
Early in the season, in June, there is a lot of snow on the trail. It can get tricky.
In July and August, rain is a common occurrence. This makes the boulder sections slippery.
Heavy rains also significantly increase the force of water at river crossings. This makes river crossings even more challenging.
Please note: The safety of our trekkers is paramount. Our technical team, in conjunction with the Trek Leader, constantly monitors the weather conditions and makes informed decisions about proceeding with or aborting the trek. If the weather deteriorates, the team may need to turn back from any campsite to ensure the safety of all trekkers.
Altitude:
The Miyar Valley trek starts rather high at 11,564 ft. During the trek, you climb to 13,202 feet. This high start makes you susceptible to altitude sickness in the first couple of days.
At altitudes above 10,000 ft, the chances of being hit by Acute Mountain Sickness are real. Altitude sickness can happen to anybody - first-time trekkers as well as experienced trekkers. Fitness helps in making your trek easier. But do not forget that even a fit person climbing too high, too soon runs an equal risk of developing AMS.
Here’s a Complete guide to Acute Mountains Sickness, HAPE and HACE.
Please Note: The Indiahikes team is equipped with high-altitude medical kits, oxygen cylinders, and stretchers to handle any altitude-related emergencies.
Emergency Exits:
Miyar Valley trek has limited exit points. In case of any injury, you need to retrace your way back to Khanjer and then travel to Udaipur, 31 km away.
Closest Hospital to the Miyar Valley Trek:
Udaipur Community Health Center is the closest hospital in case of an emergency. For better facilities, you need to travel all the way back to Manali, a 3 hr drive from Khanjer.
The Indiahikes team will ensure your safety throughout the journey. However, we believe that safety is a shared responsibility. We need your active participation and attention to safety protocols to ensure a safe and successful trek.
Please inform your Trek Leader about any pre-existing medical conditions or health concerns, no matter how minor they may seem. This will enable us to take the necessary precautions and ensure your safety throughout the trek.
```
