Moderate
Suitable for Fit Beginners
You will cover a total of around 37.6 km in five trekking days, reaching a highest altitude of over 13,000 ft above sea level. Although it is rated as a moderate trek, there are ascents, descents, and boulder sections that require proper fitness and mental preparation.
The Terrain:
The trek involves long days of trekking. There are multiple days that are almost 10 kms long. On the last day, you trek for 16 kms at a stretch. This requires good stamina and preparation.
You start traversing boulder sections on the way to your Khaari campsite. Beyond Khaari, the actual boulder fields begin and you trek through it continuously for over 3 kms. This requires strong legs and careful footing.
The descent to Patalwansar Lakes from Khari can be tricky. The next day, on your way to Dison, you need to climb back up the same boulder fields. These will test your preparation.
The initial descent to Dwar from Dison is steep and along a narrow, rocky ledge. Although this section is not too long, it can be tricky.
Please note: If you are trekking with Indiahikes, our Trek Leaders and Technical Guides will provide you with all necessary guidance, support, and technical gear such as microspikes, gaiters, helmets, ropes, etc., wherever required.
Weather:
The Gurez Valley Trek is ideal for monsoons because it sits in a rainshadow region. However, at higher altitudes, sudden rains and snows are always a distinct possibility.
Rainfall or snowfall lasting hours can change the entire trek dynamic. Rain can flood campsites or make the trails too slushy to walk safely on, while snowfall can obstruct previously made paths, requiring fresh tracks to be cut on the snow.
Please note: The safety of our trekkers is paramount. In conjunction with the Trek Leader, our technical team constantly monitors the weather conditions and makes informed decisions about proceeding with or aborting the trek. If the weather deteriorates, the team may need to turn back from any campsite to ensure the safety of all trekkers.
Altitude:
On the first day of your trek, you gain almost 3000 ft in elevation over just 5 km of trekking. You hit the 10,000 ft mark on this day itself. Such rapid elevation gain makes you very susceptible to Acute Mountain Sickness.
At altitudes above 10,000 ft, the chances of being hit by Acute Mountain Sickness are real. Altitude sickness can happen to anybody, first-time trekkers as well as experienced trekkers. Fitness helps in making your trek easier. But do not forget that even a fit person climbing too high, too soon runs an equal risk of developing AMS. Here’s a Complete guide to Acute Mountain Sickness, HAPE and HACE
Please note: The Indiahikes team is equipped with high-altitude medical kits, oxygen cylinders, and stretchers to handle any altitude-related emergencies.
Emergency Exits:
On the Gurez Valley Trek, the only exit point is Dawar. So you need to be alert and watch out for symptoms of AMS closely.
Closest Hospital to the Gurez Valley Trek:
In case of a medical emergency, the closest hospital is at Dawar. You have a medical facility to handle emergencies. For any major emergencies, Bandipora is the best place for getting any treatment.
If you require serious medical attention, head to Srinagar for better facilities.
The Indiahikes team will ensure your safety throughout the journey. However, we believe that safety is a shared responsibility. We need your active participation and attention to safety protocols to ensure a safe and successful trek.

What to Upload:
Use fitness tracking apps such as Strava, Nike Run Club, or similar to log your runs and workouts.
Paste your profile's link on your Indiahikes account (your profile has to be public).
Ensure that at least 3 runs from the last 1 month meet the fitness criteria of your specific trek.
Instances we may reject your fitness record:
If you fail to link your fitness profile
If you do not meet the required fitness level at least three times in the past month
Important Notes:
Children below 14 years are not required to submit fitness records.
However, parents must ensure that children remain physically active and prepared for the trek.
